# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** HR managers, Legal counsel, and Compliance officers at EU companies with 50+ employees. They are not optional buyers — EU Whistleblowing Directive mandates they have a system in place. They are risk-aware, time-pressed, and evaluating on trust first, features second.

**Secondary:** Compliance consultants who manage whistleblowing systems across multiple client organizations simultaneously. They need multi-tenant control and white-label branding.

**Tertiary:** Whistleblowers themselves — employees or stakeholders filing reports. They are anxious, cautious, and need to feel completely safe before they submit anything.

## Product Purpose

Phoenix is a B2B SaaS platform for secure whistleblowing and ethics reporting. It provides organizations with a fully managed, anonymous reporting system: multi-channel intake (web form, phone, SMS, chat, mobile app), case management with tagging and team collaboration, customizable branding, and data hosted on Swiss servers with GDPR compliance.

The market is compliance-driven, not optional. Success means a compliance officer looks at Phoenix and immediately feels: "This is the one I can trust, and the one I can actually get approved by legal."

## Brand Personality

Modern, credible, and quietly warm. Think Stripe's clarity and confidence, but with enough human softness that a frightened employee feels safe pressing "submit." Three words: **trusted, precise, humane**.

Voice: direct and clear. Never corporate-speak. Never alarming. The product handles serious situations — the tone should be calm and competent, not dramatic.

## Anti-references

- **IBM / SAP** — corporate-boring, form-over-feeling, cold institutional grey
- **Cybersecurity firms (CrowdStrike, Darktrace)** — hacker-dark, threat-heavy aesthetic; we protect people, not hunt threats
- **Generic SaaS purple-gradient** (Notion clones, Webflow templates, every 2023 startup) — undifferentiated, forgettable
- **Heavy legalese landing pages** — dense compliance portals that look like government websites

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before features.** Every screen should communicate safety and credibility before it lists capabilities. If a nervous employee lands on the page, they should feel reassured within 3 seconds.
2. **Warmth inside precision.** Stripe-level layout clarity, but color and copy choices that feel human — not cold, not clinical.
3. **Compliance is the context, not the product.** The EU Directive is why buyers come; Phoenix's ease and security is why they stay. Don't lead with regulation, lead with relief.
4. **Confidence without bravado.** Swiss-server security and 50+ language support are strong proof points. State them plainly; let the facts carry weight without overselling.
5. **Protect both sides.** The buyer needs to trust the vendor. The reporter needs to trust the system. Design for both simultaneously — credibility outward, safety inward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Anonymous reporting means some users may be in distress or high-stress situations — prioritize clear affordances, legible type at all sizes, and avoid patterns that create confusion or hesitation (e.g. unclear form progression, ambiguous CTAs).
